BLENDER PROJECTS

Game development is equal parts art and programming. I took up the challenge of learning to use Blender because I felt that understanding the process which art must go through to be placed into a game is integral to Game Development. Now, I have experience modeling, rigging, animating, and manipulating skeletons inside the engine.

It just so happens that art is one of my hobbies!


FOX

A model I made for my Senior Project game. He has 4 animations for each cardinal direction of movement which, using a blend tree in Unity, resulted in the appearance of 8-directional movement animations.



DRAGON

A quick, low-poly model I made of my D&D character, a Pact of the Dragon Warlock. After working hard to make 4 animations for my Fox model, I decided to reduce my work by only making a forward and backward moving animation and sort out 8-directional movement in code instead.



DRUID-NECROMANCER

My first Blender model where I hand painted the textures and worked in many fine details. She is my concept for a druid-necromancer who's fighting moves involve spirits of deceased animals.
